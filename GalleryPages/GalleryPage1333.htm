But these are not trivial issues and should be looked at objectively (for instance by reading a thermometer). The earth's average temperature has increased, in step with rising atmospheric carbon dioxide levels. Glaciers are melting and sea levels are on the rise. Climate change is real and we are the major cause. Evolution is fact, not theory. The evidence for this is incontovertible. Vaccines are a good thing, and the moon rocks are not fake. Being right matters, and science has a long track record of getting things right in the end. Science has transported us to the outer reaches of the solar system, given us push-button comfort and communication, and nearly doubled our life expectancy, all in a single century. Enough said!

Nevertheless, as stated, the truth is elusive. Therefore, this volume, in the words of Ansel Adams, is my "aggressive personal opinion" based on 50 years of study and exploration. It is not necessarily a page-by-page presentation of facts, since only mathematics can do that. Conclusions are subject to change as new information becomes available.

 

Talk about elusive truth. The Mayans developed the most advanced civilization in pre-Columbian America. They were noted astronomers and mathematicians with the only writing system in the Americas prior to European arrival. And yet their great civilization suddenly and mysteriously collapsed around 1200 CE. Despite the efforts of international scientists for more than 50 years, the cause(s) remains unknown. But recent data gathered by NASA and Smithsonian scientists, including satellite imaging, have shed new light on this mystery. These scientists assert that overpopulation led to deforestation, which in turn caused climate change and drought. Agricultural failure followed, with subsequent intertribal warfare and eventual collapse.
