There is no lack of water here unless you try to establish a city where no city should be.
Edward Abbey-"Desert Solitaire"

It's a mad, mad, mad world. If you doubt it, consider this: the American desert southwest is the fastest growing region in the country, and has been for awhile. It seems people will do most anything to escape the north-even move to Las Vegas, Phoenix, or Tucson, where they trade northern exposure for desert hyperthermia. These are boom towns all. Why in the world?

It's a hot and scary place, with bleached bones and rusty revolvers, empty beer cans and broken whiskey bottles, and dreams that seldom come true. There are rattlesnakes everywhere: diamondback monsters, python sized, with poison to boot. And more bugs than snakes, in your face, and in the trail mix. Sand in the soup and quicksand on the trail. Mice and scorpions scurrying around your tent. Out of water and miles from nowhere. The ever-so- patient vulture, circling overhead, cheering for the snakes.

Really! Why would anyone move there?
