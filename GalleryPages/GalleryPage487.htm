The agricultural revolution, without a doubt the most significant event in the history of life, is a mixed bag at best. Its great success in fueling man's procreative fire cannot be denied. Archaeologists tell us that a critical mass of humans was required to spawn the "progress" of civilization-cities, writing, monument building, metallurgy, and finally technology and industry. Therefore, if civilization is taken as the epitome of human achievement, then agriculture turns out to be a good thing.

Yet when compared to hunting and gathering in a pristine environment, repeated studies show agriculture to be more labor intensive and to provide a less balanced diet. Foraging societies have more time for recreation and matters of the spirit, lost causes in the modern world. And without agriculture, perhaps a superior alternative to the often wanton human condition might have evolved. In fact, the "civilized" art of living peacably in small communities, without forming states, was present in Africa prior to outside influence. Food for thought!
