Man's inhumanity to man is well documented, but what about to other life forms? Do we just beat up on each other or are we less selective? It would seem that our insensitivity, fueled by greed, ignorance, and ego, knows no bounds.

The largest species ever to occupy this planet is the blue whale, twice as big as the biggest dinosaur, bigger even than the General Sherman giant sequoia in California. In the early 1900's whalers, operating out of Grytviken station on the wild island outpost of South Georgia, butchered 25,000 baleen whales in less than 50 years, many of them blues. In the process they killed a speciman that was 110 feet long and weighed 200,000 pounds! The largest living creature ever to live! For blubber to light the street lamps of London.

Abraham walked the earth when certain bristlecone pines first thrust their tender roots into the two mile high gravelly soil of the White Mountains in California. These oldest pines are living ruins, fittingly stunted and gnarled to look the part. Windblown ice and sand have scoured the wood smooth and beautiful, as if worked by the hand of god. The oldest known tree is Methuselah, 4800 years, making it the oldest living thing on earth. But it wasn't always so.

Once upon a time there was Prometheus, a bristlecone pine tree killed shortly after discovery in 1964. It seems there was a geologist in search of information about Ice Age glaciers, and was willing to do anything to get it. He was taking core samples from the bristlecone pines of California when he found a tree he knew to be at least 4,000 years old. As he sampled it, his coring tool broke. Rather than wait for another tool the man asked for and received permision from the U.S. Forest Service to cut the tree down. To kill it in minutes with a chain saw! Prometheus proved to be 4,950 years, the oldest known organism to ever live on earth, 300 years old when the pyramids were built in Egypt.

Neither the scientist nor the Forest Service ever expressed remorse at this outrageous act. The public, however, did, and the identity of the Methuselah tree is thus kept secret to prevent a fate similar to Prometheus.
