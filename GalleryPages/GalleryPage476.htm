The rules of life dictate that a species adapts or else. This is true for any mutant form. Or is it?

Once upon a time there was a crafty creature that swung out of the African jungle and onto the savanna as a biped. A more significant step has never been taken. Homo erectus, by the very act of standing up, would step into history and change the face of the planet for all time.

He did it initially by following all of Darwin's rules to become the supreme adapter. Then, using his increasingly complex brain, he built shelters, crafted tools, and learned to use fire for heat and cooking. Thus would Homo sapiens become the most successful species of all time, the only one to adapt to every ecological niche on the planet. An impressive resume, no doubt! (next photograph)
