Science is not only compatible with spirituality, it is a profound source of spirituality.
Carl Sagan

Disbelief in science is alive and well today, having flourished since the Middle Ages when Galileo was threatened for daring to assert that the earth orbited the sun. This ran counter to the teachings of the church and was, shall we say, frowned upon. Today, most people profess to believe in the scientific method. But do they really? The problem is the evidence-based method can lead us to truths that are less than self-evident and at odds with our core beliefs. Then what do we do? We believe what is convenient despite mountains of evidence to the contrary. And there's always a mad scientist on the internet with "proof" that our beliefs, no matter how ludicrous, represent truth. The media love to give attention to these naysayers because it sells.

Just how ludicrous can it get? Surf the web and you can find a cult of people who are convinced the moon landings never happened. The entire space program, the race with Russia, exhibits at the Smithsonian Air and Space Museum? All a hoax. Alan Shepherd, John Glenn, and Neal Armstrong? Frauds.

In Portland, Oregon, not generally known as a hotbed of conservatism, fluoridation of the municipal water supply was voted down in 2013, and not for the first time. The stated reason? Fluoride may be harmful to human health. This assertion despite 60 years of use by most of the rest of the country, without a single confirmed case of toxicity. Even the fist pounders are mostly mum on this one. Science has shown that fluoride is a cheap, safe, and effective way to hang on to your teeth.

And don't even get me started on vaccines. Oh well, too late. One of the truly great discoveries of all time, vaccines have changed the world for the better. No other therapeutic intervention is more natural or logical. No drugs. No surgery. No sorcery. Vaccines take advantage of our own immune systems to add years, perhaps even decades, to life expectancy. They have contributed enormously to our well being. Period.

Not so long ago in the U.S. a newly conceived child had about a 50-50 shot at making it to the age of five (it's still that way in parts of Africa, Asia, and South America). Today a young child dying of anything will make the front page of most small town newspapers. Thank you very much modern world, including clean air and water, balanced nutrition, sanitation, and high tech medicine, including vaccines. And by the way Jenny McCarthy, there is not one shard of credible medical evidence, despite decades of worldwide research, that vaccines cause autism.
