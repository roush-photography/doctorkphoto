Beneath a very thin segment of the earth's crust, in northwest Wyoming, is a realm of fire and brimstone. This geological "hotspot" is responsible for the hellish landscape of geysers, hot springs, and bubbly mud that is Yellowstone, the world's first national park, established 1872.

Yellowstone! The name wreaks of enchantment. Like Disneyland and almost as popular, and as wild. At Yellowstone geysers erupt, mud boils, and hot springs sparkle. The river roars through that sulfurous canyon. Land shifts and lakes gurgle. There is a magical mystery to it all.

Besides geology there is wild nature at Yellowstone. A superstar in its own right. The Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem consists of 14 million acres of mountains, meadows, and forests. The Green, Snake, and Yellowstone rivers begin in the glorious high country and tumble over hundreds of waterfalls as they journey out of the park. Spectacular wildlife includes bison, bear, moose, and many others. An intact ecosystem, restored to balance with reintroduction of the wolf in 1995, allows us a vision of the world as it was in the beginning.
