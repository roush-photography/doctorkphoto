What is it about one photograph that makes an audience gasp while most others put them to sleep? Photography depends on the triad of light, composition, and subject, blended magically to elicit this response. The photographer seeks to communicate something powerful with his tools and vision, to provide proof that the universe is grander than we dared to dream.

Without light there is no vision or color, much less photography. How light is perceived and captured is the photographer's constant challenge and delight. We watch it illuminate, soften, and dance upon the world. In magic light there are no ordinary moments. More than any other quality it is light that makes us gasp. My mentor, Galen Rowell, ever the rebel, used to say "Look for the light and find a subject to go with it," an obvious reference to the photgrapher's obsession with subject. Rowell devoted his all too abbreviated life to capturing the essence of mountain light.

Composition is the tiny vignette a photographer places in the frame as he snaps the shutter. It encompasses the geometrical arrangement of subject and supporting elements, as well as perspective. He must reduce a big, messy world to simple, sleek terms. This is the photographer's other immediate and lifelong task.

Capturing the subject at the perfect moment is the most difficult challenge of all and, therefore, these photos are the rarest. The mass of moments are mundane. Often painful patience is required, awaiting the fleeting miracle. And then it happens-hands touch, a smile, an exclamation. A memory!
