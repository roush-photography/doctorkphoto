Man is the only species that blushes, and the only one that needs to.
Mark Twain

Many volumes, including this one, rail against the awful finality of extinction. It is forever, yet still the species fall-the ivory-billed woodpecker, the passenger pigeon, and more recently the Yangtze dolphin. And soon the bengal tiger and the monarch butterfly. And many thousands less well known and charismatic, as Homo sapiens grinds inexorably toward a monoculture world of corn and concrete.

Every voice is needed, the telling and retelling a moral absolute. Apathy is not an option, it is a death sentence for many of life's miraculous forms. We must share this marvelous passion for our animal brethren with the workaday world for whom nature remains mostly peripheral, or even worse, an adversary. We must light a candle for our fellow creatures.
