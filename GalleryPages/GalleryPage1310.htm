Only two things are infinite, the universe and human stupidity
And I'm not sure about the former
Albert Einstein

"It is clear that on environmental issues Ronald Reagan rode against the American mainstream for eight years. The 'great communicator' was unable to persuade Congress to repeal a single important environmental law. He is the only president this century to serve his entire term with no major conservation initiative." This powerful quote by Stewart Udall, former Secretary of the Interior under President Kennedy, speaks volumes about the environmental backlash of the eighties, engineered by Ronald Reagan and his reactionary Interior Secretary, James Watt.

The Secretary would take the reins of office and lead a charge to increase logging, drilling, and mining on public lands. This was shortly after proclaiming to the nation that environmentalism was a "left-wing cult," perhaps his most infamous quote, but not his downfall. That would come a short time later, after a speech to the U.S. Chamber of Commerce when Watt mocked affirmative action with the following statement about a coal leasing panel: "I have a black, a woman, two Jews, and a cripple. And we have talent too." In one fell swoop the Secretary revealed himself as the model of stupidity (and bigotry) Professor Einstein warned us about! He subsequently resigned in disgrace. Time magazine named him one of the ten worst cabinet appointees in history.

As if James Watt wasn't enough to deal with, the decade of the eighties was punctuated with truly catastrophic events. A congressional study of 1984 found that thousands of lakes and miles of streams in the northern U.S. (as well as Europe and Asia) were nearly lifeless as a result of acid rain. Airborne pollution from factories near and far was the culprit. The same year a toxic leak at the Union Carbide plant in Bhopal, India resulted in the worst industrial disaster in history, killing 2,000 and injuring 200,000 people. A few years later the worst nuclear accident in history occurred at Chernobyl, in the Ukraine. A half million residents were relocated and as many workers were mobilized in a futile effort to contain and clean up the damage. The cost in health is still being calculated and the truth will never be known.

The first tentative evidence of human related global warming was published in the mid eighties, followed shortly by further evidence of an expanding ozone hole over Antarctica. True to administration form Reagan's Energy Secretary Donald Hodel suggested darker sunglasses and more sunscreen as a solution to the problems. When the public relations backlash settled from this absurdity the adminstration briefly came to its senses and joined with 23 other nations in a phase-out of CFCs.

 

Climate change is obvious in Alaska as glaciers melt and temperatures rise. In 2016 the humpback whales began their migration from Alaska to Hawaii three months later than usual. One of the possible explanations is global warming.
