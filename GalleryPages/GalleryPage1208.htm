The one process now going on that will take millions of years to correct is loss of species diversity
by destruction of natural habitats. This is the folly our descendents are least likely to forgive us.
E.O. Wilson

So extinction is a natural, necessary, and ongoing process, taking place quietly, with little fanfare, like two species passing in the night. However, mass extinctions, such as the event that made room for you and me (see previous page), is something else entirely. These are catastrophic events that can take life back to its amoeboid beginnings. In the broadest sense they are natural and normal, but definitely not quiet.

Since life began on earth, four billion years ago, there have been five known mass extinctions when most planetary life was eliminated by a tumultuous force. We're not talking about a species or two. The key word here is most. The last of these extinctions took place 65 million years ago and until recently was a mystery to scholars. We now know that the Age of Reptiles, about 100 million years of earth time, came to a screeching halt when a massive asteroid collided with the planet off the Yucatan coast of Mexico. This earth shattering event led to tsunamis, fire storms, and cataclysmic climate upheaval that selected for mammals over reptiles. T. rex never had a chance.

The sixth mass extinction is currently in progress and is unique. It is the only one driven, not by geology or astronomy, but by a single LIVING force-Homo sapiens! The inescapable truth is astonishing. Through direct persecution or habitat destruction humankind will bring about the demise of 25 to 50% of planetary species in the current century, a geological millisecond. We are the most awesome force for change the earth has ever known, greater even than asteroids and volcanoes. We became this way mostly in the last century through population growth and an acceleration of the technological revolution. As a result we exponentially increased our power to burn, cut, dig, move, and otherwise transform the physical earth. And that has given us all the advantages of the modern world while at the same time making us the most potent extinction force in 65 million years. Maybe of all time. We are king of the mountain, for now.
